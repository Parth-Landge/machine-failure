MILLING MACHINE MAINTENANCE & TROUBLESHOOTING MANUAL

SECTION 1: HIGH TOOL WEAR (>200 mins)
- Diagnosis: The cutting tool has degraded past safe operational limits.
- Action: Immediately halt the spindle. Replace the milling cutter. Recalibrate the Z-axis offset.
- Warning: Continued operation will cause severe workpiece damage and potential spindle bearing failure.

SECTION 2: TEMPERATURE ANOMALIES (Process Temp > 310K or Temp Difference < 8K)
- Diagnosis: Insufficient coolant flow or extreme friction detected.
- Action: Check coolant reservoir levels. Inspect coolant nozzles for blockages. Reduce Rotational Speed (RPM) by 15%. 
- Warning: Thermal expansion may cause tool binding.

SECTION 3: TORQUE OVERLOAD (Torque > 60 Nm)
- Diagnosis: The machine is cutting too deep or the material is too hard for the current RPM.
- Action: Reduce feed rate. Increase RPM to lower chip load. Check if the tool is blunt.
